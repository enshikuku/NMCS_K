Brief History about NMCS-K

NMCS-Kenya first began as International Movement of Catholic Students (IMCS) which was accredited in 1921 in Fribourg Switzerland under the name PAX ROMANA. IMCS-PAX ROMANA is the successor to the international union of catholic students in Fribourg Switzerland. It was approved by a letter from cardinal Gasparri to Max Gressly president of the International Union of Catholic Students on 5th June 1921. Because of its powerful witness to reconciliation and solidarity under the motto of ‘PAX CHRISTI IN REGNO CHRISTI’ the union began to be known as “Pax Romana”. Thus IMCS-PAX ROMANA as an organization of the student apostolate is a federation of national movements and associations of catholic student in higher education, without prejudice to their autonomy. In Kenya, the movement was established in the year 1995 at Kenyatta University from where it spread to other tertiary institutions.
Later on in Kenya, the movement changed its name officially to PAX ROMANA-NATIONAL MOVEMENT OF CATHOLIC STUDENTS-KENYA.. The national secretariat of NMCS Kenya is based at the Kenya National Catholic Youth Centre at Mji wa Furaha, Nairobi

OUR MOTTO AND METHODOLOGY

NMCS-Kenya is guided by the motto "Preferential option for the poor." It is the poor that Jesus Christ himself identified with.
Our methodology is "Reflect-act-reflect." It is imperative that we take action on asituation, we reflect on it first, then act and after that we asses our actions by reflecting

Objectives

Pax Romana NMCS Kenya is committed to the following objectives:

  1. To promote the student apostolate among students in higher education stressing their responsibility in life, in the church's mission and in the world.
  2. To recognize the problems of justice a recognition which is vital to the transformation of society and work with all concerned women and men for joint action and reflection leading to a more just and equitable social order at all levels: national, continental and international.
  3. To further the Christian faith experiences lived in one's own commitment and try to communicate this faith to the student milieu by bearing witness to Jesus Christ.
  4. To encourage pedagogies of action which help students integrate their Christian faith in their efforts to build a more just society.

With this objectives, Pax Romana NMCS Kenya tries to:

  1. Facilitate formation of catholic students' movements and associations in countries in which they do not yet exist and to see that their continued growth goes hand in hand with that of the existing movements
  2. Provide the students and chaplains of member movements and associations with possibilities for meeting and reflecting on joint problems, on the basis of the situation in their own continents, institutions, as well as on international problems.
  3. Propose guidelines for actions to be carried out in solidarity by the member movements and associations in accordance with the possibilities and reality of each national group.
  4. Organize, within the framework of its international secretariat, an efficient communication service for the exchange of information in all areas concerning the student apostolates' sphere competence in the institutions of higher education.
  5. To represent, at the international level, the catholic student movement in university and tertiary institutions and to cooperate with other international organization.

Activities and Events

As a movement, we take part in various activities at chapter level and also at National level. Most of our activities are charity-driven. As a movement, we have adopted more than 15 children nation wide and they are under the care of the respective chapters. To support the children, chapters often organise fund drives to raise money for school fees for the children. Apart from that, our members also take part in tree planting and environmental conservation. Above all, at the end of the day we all come together to pray and fellowship. Our events are mostly organized by the National council (National Executive and the chapter representatives). There are about four national events that are organized every year by the National office.
